How Import Duties Work

When you import goods into a country, the government charges an import duty (also called a tariff or customs duty) on the value of those goods. The rate depends on two things: where the goods come from (origin country) and what they are (product classification).

Your total landed cost is the true cost of getting a product to your warehouse: product cost + shipping + insurance + import duties. Most merchants underestimate this number because they forget about duties — until they get an unexpected customs bill.

In 2026, the tariff landscape is particularly volatile. US-China tariffs range from 20-104% depending on the product. The EU has retaliatory tariffs on US goods. Canada imposed counter-tariffs. New bilateral deals (Japan 15%, Vietnam 20%) add complexity. This calculator helps you navigate all of it.

What is CIF Value?

CIF stands for Cost, Insurance, and Freight. Most countries calculate import duty on the CIF value — not just the product cost. That means your shipping and insurance costs increase the duty you pay. Our calculator accounts for this automatically.
