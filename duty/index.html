What this import duty calculator helps you estimate

This free calculator applies one historical country-level rate assumption to a simplified planning base. It can produce a rate assumption, modeled duty, and estimated subtotal from origin, destination, product value, and any optional freight or insurance inputs.

The estimated subtotal is product value plus optional freight and insurance plus modeled duty. It excludes taxes, brokerage, carrier and processing fees, special remedies, and other costs. Validate all of those items before customs filing or final pricing.

Quick answer

Use this snapshot to compare rough scenarios only. Product classification, entry date, tariff layers, preferences, exclusions, valuation rules, taxes, and fees can change the final amount.

Freight and insurance are optional inputs

Adding freight or insurance here is a planning convention, not a statement that either amount is always dutiable. The customs-value rules depend on the destination and transaction; confirm the legally dutiable base for the actual entry.

Legacy planning coverage

The snapshot contains country-level assumptions for five destination markets: US, Canada, EU, UK, and Australia. It was updated March 18, 2026 with evidence verified only through March 13, 2026, and it has no preserved row-level derivation.
